Childhood Teenage College

Sean Kirby E. Reales • 12.18.25

Childhood Days

1Alright, let's start at the very beginning, shall we? 2My name is Sean Kirby E. Reales, I was born in Manila and was raised in Parañaque. 3My family lived in a small house, the size of a typical studio apartment but I was happy enough to live in it. 4I was a typical kid in the slums, I remember waking up to the sound of airplanes passing by every morning since we were living nearby the airport. 5The noise used to annoy me so much since it wakes me up and even made the TV signal weak. 6My parents worked hard to provide for me so I was living very content with my life even though we were poor at the time. 7I spent my afternoons like a typical kid in 2000s, playing with my childhood friends. 8We played all sorts of games like sipa, tumbang preso, langit lupa, etc. 9Sometimes, we also played inside of my house, playing monopoly or a random lego pieces box that my grandpa's boss gave to me. 10Those two toys/games really developed my creativity and logical thinking. 11On certain days though, I was stuck keeping an eye on the sari-sari store that my grandparents had, which frustrated me because I wanted to play instead. 12Looking back, I now realize they were teaching me responsibility even if I didn’t appreciate it then. 13School was a short walk from our house (maybe 5-10 minutes) and I enjoyed walking with my friends. 14I liked reading books ever since I was a kid, it was all I ever did to pass time in school. 15I wouldn’t say I was exceptionally smart, but I consistently ranked within the top five of my class throughout elementary school. 16Despite that, I was a lazy, forgetful, and easily distracted from my studies. 17I remember my uncle/aunt trying to teach me math and I always ended up sleeping midway through it. 18They never believed me when I said I didn’t have homework because I forgot it so often. 19I even had to ask a nearby classmate to confirm assignments, and most of the time I did have homework, I just somehow forgot about it. 20These experiences made my childhood so memorable.

Teenage Years

1This period had a drastic change from my childhood which was so happy whereas my teenage years was full of challenges. 2My parents separated and I had to move away with my mom and her brother/my uncle from everything and everyone that I ever knew up to that point. 3At first, I didn't know what happened and just sorta welcomed the change but this adjustment was far harsher than I ever imagined. 4My mom and uncle was often away for work so I was left alone at home most of the time. 5Looking back, I was really lonely but didn't want them to worry about me so I just distracted myself with computer and video games. 6High school wasn't much better either since puberty all that comes with it. 7I was bullied since I was a new kid and my classmates already knew each other so I had a rough time fitting in. 8I did fit in later on and it was somewhat enjoyable. 9However, when I moved up a grade, everyone that I was close with was put into a different section and I had to adjust with new people again. 10The constant bullying and adjustment occurred year after year and I ended up having depression and social anxiety due to it. 11My mom and uncle thought that it was the environment that was the issue, so in grade 10, I moved to a private school and same issue still happened. 12By then, I was so tired of everything and I wasn't going to school without my family knowing, going to a computer shop to pass the time. 13I eventually got caught and we decided that I stop going to school, which was a relief at first. 14After months passed by, I ended up thinking to myself "is the rest of my life going to be spent as bum?" and I resolved to prevent that future. 15Luckily, my mom and uncle was working from home for a company that was based in the US and they were hiring for the holidays. 16I thought to myself that I could easily do what they are doing at work when I was watching them so I said that I wanna work since I had nothing better to do and they referred me to the company. 17The company ended up hiring me for $10/hr without any interviews or anything, all I had to do was pass my resume but the condition was for my mom to train me directly, which was easily done. 18This period was full of character development to say the least, after 2 years of working non-stop (had two jobs and was working like 60-80 hour work week), I learned responsibility, mentality, and everything that made me the way I am today. 19Even though I was pretty successful compared to my peers there was one regret that is still lingering in my mind, which was graduating high school. 20I resigned my 2nd job to finish my education and to my surprise, I was doing pretty good at high school now compared to before, I was popular, excelled at my studies due to my work ethics, and graduated with high honors.

The On-Going CE

1My college life is still ongoing, and as a second-year, second-semester computer science student. 2I am continuing to learn, grow, and adjust to the demands of academic life. 3Entering college felt like a new beginning after everything I had been through, and it gave me a sense of direction. 4At first, I struggled with the workload and the pressure to perform well, but I slowly learned how to manage my responsibilities more effectively. 5My previous work experience helped me develop discipline, which made handling deadlines and academic tasks easier than before. 6I discovered that college requires not just intelligence but consistency, patience, and proper time management. 7I met new people who made the experience less overwhelming and more enjoyable. 8Although I am still not the most sociable person, I found classmates who made me feel supported. 9The subjects I am taking are not always easy, especially those involving programming and mathematics, but I do my best to improve little by little. 10My professors encourage critical thinking, which challenges me but also motivates me. 11There are days when balancing school, work responsibilities, and personal life becomes difficult. 12Still, I remind myself how far I have come from the person who once struggled to even attend class. 13College also allowed me to explore my interests and develop new skills I never thought I would need. 14Every semester feels like another step toward building the future I want for myself. 15Even though I am not done yet, I already feel proud of the progress I have made. 16I know that finishing college will not just be a personal achievement but a symbol of everything I overcame. 17I am committed to completing my degree and making the most out of the remaining years ahead of me. 18Although the path is still long, I am determined to keep moving forward. 19My ongoing college journey continues to shape me into someone stronger, more disciplined, and more hopeful for what lies beyond graduation. 20Here's to two more years of college, cheers! 21small>2nd YR CS student.
